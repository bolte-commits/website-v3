Why DEXA Scan?

Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DEXA) is the gold standard for measuring body composition. Here's why it's the best choice:

  • High Accuracy: DEXA scans are used in research labs to provide the most precise measurement of body fat, muscle mass, and bone density.
  • Comprehensive Analysis: Get detailed breakdowns of your entire body composition, not just overall percentages.
  • Regional Analysis: Understand body composition in different regions (arms, legs, trunk) to identify imbalances.
  • Visceral Fat Measurement: Accurately measure visceral fat (fat around organs), a critical health indicator linked to metabolic disease risk.
  • Bone Health: Receive valuable information about your bone density, important for long-term health.
  • Metabolism Insights: Body composition data helps estimate your resting metabolic rate (RMR) and overall metabolic health.
  • Track Progress: Perfect for monitoring changes in muscle mass and fat loss over time with consistent measurements.
  • Low Radiation: DEXA uses minimal radiation - less than a standard X-ray.
  • Quick & Non-Invasive: The scan takes just 5-7 minutes and requires no preparation.

Unlike other methods like bioelectrical impedance or calipers, DEXA provides laboratory-grade accuracy that can help you make informed decisions about your fitness and health journey.

How to Prepare

Preparing for your DEXA scan is simple. Follow these guidelines for the most accurate results:

Before Your Scan:

  • Fasting Not Required: You can eat and drink normally before your scan.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water as usual - dehydration won't affect results.
  • No Exercise Needed: You don't need to exercise before the scan.
  • Wear Light Clothing: Wear comfortable, lightweight clothing without metal buttons or zippers.

During Your Scan:

  • Lie Still: You'll lie comfortably on a scanning table for 5-7 minutes.
  • Stay Relaxed: The scan is completely painless and non-invasive.
  • Breathe Normally: No special breathing techniques required.

Important Notes:

  • If you're pregnant or think you might be pregnant, please inform us before your scan.
  • Remove all jewelry and metal objects before the scan.
  • If you have any medical implants or devices, let us know in advance.

That's it! No complicated preparation needed. Just arrive at your scheduled time and we'll take care of the rest.
